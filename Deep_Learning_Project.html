Project Overview

In the Fall Semester of 2024, I worked on a three-month project focused on weather forecasting in dry regions. My goal was to address the challenge of accurately predicting precipitation in arid areas, where even small amounts of rainfall can significantly impact agriculture. Reliable forecasts in these regions are crucial, as they can help optimize water usage and support crop planning.

I began by building a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) model, which leverages its unique ability to remember historical patterns through its gated memory structure. Next, I implemented a Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) model, which streamlines the computational process by using fewer parameters while maintaining the ability to handle sequential data effectively. Finally, the best-performing model was a Transformer with four attention heads, which achieved a remarkable accuracy of 90% for predicting precipitation values of 2 millimeters.
